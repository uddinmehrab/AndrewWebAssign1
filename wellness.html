Wellness

We know you have heard this many times, but it is very important to take care of your physical, social, and mental wellbeing while at university.

Physical Well-Being Social Well-Being Mental Well-Being

Physical Well-Being

Eat Well: Food can greatly impact your energy levels, mood, and academic performance. Try to avoid fast food every day, drink several glasses of water, and minimize sugary foods.

Move Well: Grab a friend and explore Western’s trails or visit the rec center. Physical activity improves brain function and can help with exams.

Sleep Well: Stick to a regular bedtime if possible. Avoid caffeine or alcohol before bed, keep a consistent wake-up time, and create a quiet, dark, cool environment for sleep.

Social Well-Being

Engage Well: Consider joining a club or volunteering. If clubs aren’t for you, volunteering can be another way to connect with people on campus.

Chat Well: Find friends with shared interests and stay in touch with family and friends online. If you’re in residence, try spending time in common areas to meet new people.

Mental Well-Being

Think Well: Focus on what you can control. When overwhelmed, find one manageable task and complete it. Breaking down tasks can reduce stress.

Breathe Well: Identify what brings you joy (e.g., reading, music, or nature) and make time for it. When anxious, try deep breathing or stretch by a sunny window.
